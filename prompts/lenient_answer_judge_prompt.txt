You are judging whether a model answer is leniently consistent with a reference answer for a financial QA dataset.

Task:
- Compare the candidate answer against the reference answer.
- Be lenient about harmless formatting differences.
- Focus on whether the candidate captures the same final meaning or value as the reference.

Inputs:
- Question: {question}
- Context: {context}
- Reference answer: {reference_answer}
- Candidate answer: {candidate_answer}
- Candidate reasoning: {candidate_reasoning}

Judging rules:
1. Mark `is_consistent=true` when the answers mean the same thing, even if formatting differs.
2. Treat these as consistent when the semantic answer is the same:
   - minor rounding differences
   - percentage formatting differences such as `0.7414` vs `74.14%` when the question clearly asks for a percent/rate/share/proportion
   - commas, currency symbols, trailing zeros, or short unit-free numeric formatting
   - short equivalent labels such as `yes` vs `Yes`
3. Treat these as inconsistent:
   - opposite sign or direction
   - different ratio basis or wrong denominator/numerator
   - materially different numeric value
   - abstentions like `cannot be determined` when the reference gives a definite answer
   - explanation text whose final answer disagrees with the reference
4. If the candidate includes explanation, judge by its final stated answer, not by earlier intermediate numbers.
5. Use the question to decide whether a percent, ratio, raw number, yes/no answer, or label is expected.
6. Do not reward vague answers. If the candidate is underspecified and does not clearly match the reference, mark inconsistent.

Output strict JSON only:
{{
  "is_consistent": true,
  "judgment": "exact_match | lenient_match | inconsistent",
  "mismatch_type": "none | rounding | percent_decimal_equivalent | format_only | sign_error | calculation_error | abstain_error | text_mismatch | underspecified",
  "normalized_reference": "...",
  "normalized_candidate": "...",
  "reason": "one short sentence"
}}
